Hi,

I am Zheyuan Zhang, born in Lanzhou, Gansu province, China. I am now a final-year PhD student at Dyson School of Design Engineering, Imperial College London. I graduated from Tongji University in 2017 and got my postgraduate degree in Innovation Design Engineering (IDE) at the Royal College of Art and Imperial College London in 2019. 

 

My current research and creative focus is on human wellbeing, mental health, participatory design, cross-cultural design and human-computer interaction. My PhD thesis is on designing an embodied digital interaction for the wellbeing of healthcare professionals. 

 

In addition to my research, I am passionate about digital art and product design. I co-founded Studio Inneract, a London-based studio that explores the combination of psychological theories, technology, and interactive digital art. Also I have been doing industrial and product design since my Bachelor's degree.

Cutia

Design Research · Design Engineering · UX Design · 2018

Cutia is a phobia treatment online platform using AI-generated cartoon-style insect images to perform exposure therapy. An interactive process is proposed where realistic insect images gradually reveal, fully under the user's control.

The project leverages dcGAN to produce personalised cute-style images, countering fear and disgust with cuteness. Three stages of phobia treatment are proposed within the system.

Whisper

Product Design Engineering · UX Design · 2018

Whisper aims to provide collective feedback and analytical reflection on social media usage, raising awareness of negative personal and societal impact through active but subtle interaction.

The Shell device 'whispers' keywords when held near the ear. By shaking it, users swap whispers with random people worldwide — breaking echo chambers and embracing randomness.

Crescent

Product Design Engineering · 2016

Inspired by the shape of a new Moon, the Crescent speaker generates music for meditation based on respiratory data from wearables that detect breath frequency and stability.

Through speed, volume, and pauses in generated music, the system provides gentle feedback about respiration.

Comtour

Product Design · Metalwork · 2017

Comtour is my Bachelor’s graduation project, a dual-mode electric bike for city commuters. It is equipped with two batteries to support weekday lightweight commuting and weekend-long range trips. 

Designed by Zheyuan Zhang.
Prototype in Collaboration with Shanghai Yongjiu (永久) Bike.

Codex

Mechanical Engineering · Hardware · 2018

Codex is a light and sound installation responsive to voice and proximity, creating complex patterns using refractive parts. An exploration in future human-machine interaction through light and shadow.

Dovetail Stool

Product Design · Wood Work · 2016

Supported with only dovetail joints — the ideal choice for highlighting the ingenuity of wood joinery technique and celebrating the beauty of traditional Chinese craftsmanship.

1011 Chair

Product Design · Wood Work · 2017

1 Chair, 0 metal ware, 1 same part, 1 same joint. Designed for simplicity — minimising manufacturing, transportation cost and material waste with three identical parts.

Flow

Permanent Installation · West Middlesex University Hospital · Studio Inneract

Flow is an immersive, interactive digital art installation permanently featured at West Middlesex University Hospital. It creates emotionally engaging, healing visual experiences for patients, visitors, and staff.

Featured in The Art Newspaper, Yahoo, Health Digital Tech, Art Daily. Partners: Imperial College London, CW+ Charity, Royal College of Art, UCL.

Rippling Senses

DDW 2022 · Chelsea & Westminster Hospital · Studio Inneract

Rippling Senses explores multi-sensory, immersive experiences designed to promote emotional wellbeing in clinical settings. Exhibited at Dutch Design Week 2022 and installed at Chelsea and Westminster Hospital.

Genesis Screens

Saatchi Gallery · Chelsea & Westminster Hospital · Studio Inneract

Shown at Saatchi Gallery as part of "The Healing Art" group exhibition (December 2021 – February 2022), and also featured at Chelsea and Westminster Hospital as a permanent healing art installation.
