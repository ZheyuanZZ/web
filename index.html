Flow

Interactive Digital Installation, Permanent Feature in West Middlesex University Hospital, London

Flow is an interactive digital installation that recreates the natural movement and flow of water. Inspired by the natural movement of water, Flow mimics the ebb and flow of tides, dynamically adjusting its visuals based on the surrounding environment and the inputs from audiences. Flow adapts and shifts according to real-time environmental data such as time, temperature, ambient light, and noise levels. For example, as time shifts throughout the day, the installation changes its colour and intensity, offering a different visual experience from morning to night.
Meanwhile, ss an interactive and ever-shifting visual art, Flow responds to audience inputs, which include bodily movements and facial expressions. It invites the audience to contemplate and become aware of their bodies and emotions. The generation and strength of the waves, along with the color and visual complexity, shift dynamically according to the user’s current movements and emotions, cultivating mindfulness of both body and mind.
Flow transforms the hospital entrance into a responsive, immersive space that helps visitors, patients, and staff connect with their surroundings and adjust their moods. By interacting with the environment and its people, the installation fosters mindfulness and provides a calming visual experience amidst the fast-paced hospital atmosphere.
